Dearest Avanthika,

From random conversations to inside jokes, from exam panic sessions to endless laughs, somehow a friendship I never saw coming became one of my favorite parts of Infosys.

You simply let me be myself, and that's something I value more than you probably realize. Looking back now, that trip feels like the beginning of so many memories.

And of course, how can I forget our promise rings with Keerthana? It sounds like such a small thing, but it's one of those memories that instantly makes me smile whenever I think about it.

You've also done something else without even realizing it—you made me see people differently. You taught me that people are always more interesting than the labels and assumptions we place on them.

Thank you for being one of the people who made Infosys feel like more than just an internship. When I look back at that chapter of my life, I don't think I'll remember the classes first. I'll remember the people, the laughs, the breaks, the chaos, and the memories.

And somehow, you're at the center of so many of them.

You're always taking care of everyone around you. You're always listening, supporting, helping, and making sure the people you love are okay. But I hope this year you remember that you deserve that same care too.

You're 22 now, babe. It's time to give yourself the same kindness, patience, and love that you so freely give to others. I hope you learn to choose yourself without guilt, prioritize your own happiness, and allow yourself the space to heal.

I need you to put this in your head like a manifestation: heal yourself first before trying to heal everyone else. You deserve happiness, peace, and growth just as much as the people you're constantly looking after.

You don't always have to be the strong one. You don't always have to carry everything alone. Sometimes it's okay to slow down, breathe, and choose yourself.

As you step into another year of your life, I hope you continue living on your own terms. I hope you choose happiness over expectations, confidence over doubt, and yourself over the opinions of people who don't matter.

Keep being the person who makes everyone around her feel seen.

Keep being the friend who turns ordinary moments into memorable ones.

Keep being unapologetically yourself.

Thank you for being such an important part of my Infosys journey and for giving me memories that I know I'll look back on years from now and still smile about.

Happy Birthday once again, Avanthika.

Here's to more trips, more laughter, more inside jokes, more random Tamil lessons, more promise rings, and hopefully many more memories together.

With lots of love,
